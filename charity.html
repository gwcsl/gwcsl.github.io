Charity- ElfCon

Elves love hot cocoa, but ElfCon is also about sharing and helping others- kids help kids and that includes kids in need. I may be the Chief Elf but I’m still a kid. And don’t you want to help a kid? You sure do! And when you donate that warm and fuzzy feeling won’t be from the hot cocoa-- you just did a very good thing. Give yourself a nice hearty elf pat on the shoulder.
This is ElfCon’s first year and ElfCon is supporting the following charities (each approved hot cocoa location agreed to donate a portion of their ElfCon hot cocoa sales to ElfCon’s charities):

Children's Brain Tumor Foundation

The Children’s Brain Tumor Foundation (CBTF) was founded in 1988 by a group of dedicated parents, physicians, and friends to improve the treatment, quality of life, and the long term outlook for children with brain and spinal cord tumors through research support, education, and advocacy to families and survivors. In addition to providing funds to research a cure, the organization is the nation’s leader in quality of life programs for families impacted by brain and spinal cord tumors. They support families from the day of diagnosis and throughout the brain tumor journey. Their mission is dedicated to improving the treatment, quality of life and the long-term outlook for children and families affected by a brain or spinal cord tumor.

Stockings with Care

Stockings with Care was founded in 1992 by Rosalie Joseph and Tom Fontana. Every year since then they have come together to make this extraordinary thing happen. They are a grassroots organization run almost entirely by volunteers. It's a not-for-profit organization dedicated to making sure that families in need can create the magic of Christmas morning for their kids. Last year, Stockings with Care played Santa to over 1,500 kids. For them, it‘s about opening hearts, inspiring dreams and preserving the dignity of parents and their families.

The Trevor Project

Founded in 1998 by the creators of the Academy Award®-winning short film TREVOR, The Trevor Project is the leading national organization providing crisis intervention and suicide prevention services to lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer & questioning (LGBTQ) young people under 25.

The New York Center for Children

For 26 years, The New York Center for Children has provided free, bilingual, trauma-focused, comprehensive evaluation and therapy services to victims of child abuse and their families, for as long as their healing requires. NYCC is also a leader in training programs on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of child abuse, attended by professionals and graduate-level medical, psychology and social work students.

The Youth Mental Health Project and Awareness Program

The Youth Mental Health Project is a grassroots nonprofit organization whose mission is to educate, empower, and support families and communities to better understand and care for the mental health of our youth. They envision a world made up of informed families and caring communities that realize and strengthen the mental health of our youth — a world where people are just as aware of their mental health as their physical health, where families are validated and able to readily access the care they need.

 

How do I sign up for ElfCon?

Donate $15 to ElfCon’s charity drive and show up at the start point (posted on ElfCon’s social media @officialelfcon on Twitter and Instagram) in a costume.
 

Where will my $15 go?

Your money will be split among the charities shown above.
 

What do I get for my donation?

The personal satisfaction of donating to charity, special hot cocoa deals in the form of an Elf Approved stamp, AND an ElfCon passport, the completion of which will get you an awesome prize.
 

I’m too confused to donate online, what do I do?

There will be elves on hand to collect donations, but please do it online, it’s much easier. Elves don’t particularly like to handle cash. Just click here and follow the simple instructions.
 

How to I get my ElfCon passport and my Elf Approved stamp?

Donate to ElfCon’s charity drive and bring your email confirmation to the event. Find a volunteer, show them your email receipt, and they will direct you to where you can pick up your passport and stamp.
